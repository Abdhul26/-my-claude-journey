**Post Copy:**

Same 30-day roadmap request. Two prompts. Two completely different results.

Most people think AI gives bad answers. Usually it's just missing context.

❌ **Weak Prompt:**
"Create a 30-day learning roadmap. Include weekly milestones, daily tasks, resources, projects, and final outcome."

Result: Generic, vague, could apply to anyone. "Learn programming basics." No target role. No proof of readiness.

✅ **Improved Prompt:**
"Create a 30-day learning roadmap. Context: I'm a working professional switching into backend engineering, I know basic scripting + some SQL, my goal is a backend role in the UAE (~21K AED) within 3-6 months, I have 2-3 hrs/day, I'm Beginner-Intermediate, and I prefer projects + reading over videos. Include weekly milestones, daily tasks, resources, projects, and final outcome."

Result: Every week ties to the actual goal. Week 2 goes deep on SQL indexing and normalization — because that's what UAE backend interviews actually test. Week 4 rehearses "walk me through your project" — because that's the real interview question.

Same skeleton. Same 4 sections. Completely different value.

**The lesson:** Context doesn't just improve tone — it decides _which information makes the cut._
