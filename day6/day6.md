🎯 Day [X] of #AbtalksClaudeChallenge — Prompting Claude to Build an ATS-Optimized Resume
Most people ask AI to "improve my resume" and get generic fluff back. The real skill is telling the AI exactly what output structure, constraints, and truth-boundaries you need.
Here's the difference:
❌ Weak prompt:
"Can you make my resume better and ATS friendly?"
✅ Improved prompt:
"Rewrite my resume for ATS parsing. Output exactly two parts: (1) an ATS score comparison with bullet reasons, (2) the optimized resume as a single-column, one-page A4 PDF with standard section headers. Use only information from my original resume — never invent achievements or metrics."
The second version gives the AI a job description, not a vague wish. It defines: output format, page limit, truthfulness boundary, and section structure — so there's zero ambiguity in what "better" means.
💡 The lesson: AI doesn't guess your standards. It follows the structure you give it. The more explicit your constraints, the less editing you do afterward.
