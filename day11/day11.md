**Key takeaways for you**

1. **ATS matching is about vocabulary, not new skills.** Most of your score gain came from restating things you already do in the JD's exact language — e.g. "GitHub Actions" → also say "Git" and "version control" explicitly. Recruiters' ATS tools and human skimmers both pattern-match on keywords, not synonyms.

2. **Ownership language matters as much as tech stack.** Bullets like "end-to-end ownership: system design → deployment" map directly onto full-stack/cross-functional expectations even when every line item underneath is backend. This is worth remembering for interviews too — "I owned it end-to-end" signals seniority even in a backend-only role.

3. **Never fabricate to match a JD.** If a role wants React and you don't have it, the right move is either (a) skip that JD, (b) apply and be upfront in the interview, or (c) go learn just enough to speak to it honestly. Padding a resume gets caught at the first technical question.

4. **Gap analysis is a diagnostic tool, not just a resume exercise.** The "Missing Skills" list (front-end frameworks, CSS) is also your study list if you want to actually widen into full-stack roles rather than just relabeling backend roles.
